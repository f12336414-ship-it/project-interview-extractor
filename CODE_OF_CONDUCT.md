# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a welcoming, safe, and constructive community for people of every background, identity, experience level, and perspective.

## Expected behavior

- Communicate with empathy and technical clarity.
- Critique ideas and artifacts, not people.
- Respect privacy, attribution, and project confidentiality.
- Accept correction gracefully and help others learn.
- Disclose relevant conflicts of interest.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or sexualized attention.
- Personal attacks, deliberate intimidation, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing private information, credentials, or confidential project material without permission.
- Misrepresenting another person's work or experience.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make participation unsafe.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other spaces where someone represents this project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report incidents to the maintainer through the contact options on the [maintainer's GitHub profile](https://github.com/f12336414-ship-it). If confidentiality is required, use the repository's private vulnerability-reporting form and prefix the title with `[Code of Conduct]`.

The maintainer may remove content, issue warnings, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants. Reports will be handled as privately and promptly as the available platform permits. Retaliation against a reporter is prohibited.

## Enforcement guidelines

Responses will be proportional to impact and pattern:

1. **Correction** — private or public clarification for an isolated minor issue.
2. **Warning** — formal notice with expected corrective behavior.
3. **Temporary restriction** — time-limited loss of interaction privileges.
4. **Permanent ban** — removal for severe or repeated violations.

This code is inspired by established open-source community standards and is maintained as part of this repository.
